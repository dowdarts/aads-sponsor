Our Story

The Heart of the CGC

From a Garage to a Network

AADS was born in Cecil's Garage in Moncton. Known as the Cecil's Garage Championship (CGC), it began with a small group of players meeting Friday nights for the love of the sport. We didn't have sponsors or grants. We used square boards and tiny 50/50 draws to buy boards and trophies. Matthew Dow took that local spark and used his skills in graphic design and promotion to build a professional platform for the players.

The Truth About Our Funding

This program is a labor of love. Matthew Dow and Cecil Dow do not take a profit. In fact, we currently fund the heating costs of winter events, the DartConnect fees, the cameras, and the entire live stream setup from our own personal pockets. We do this because we believe the players deserve a stage and the community needs a home. We offer exposure to our partners not to get rich, but to keep the series running and keep the sport alive.

Building Stage Presence

Matthew designed AADS after traveling to the Canadian Open and seeing talented regional players underperform because they were "stage struck." We provide the lights, the cameras, and the atmosphere so that when our players reach the National stage, they've already played like pros.

Community Impact

We Take Care of Our Own

AADS is a platform for the people. In our first year, we raised over $10,000 for those in need.

The Carl Zugay Medical Fund

Carl is a 30-year veteran of the local leagues. When a medical condition left him struggling to walk, he mentioned a leg brace he couldn't afford. Matthew along with the CGC family rallied the community. In a single night, we raised $1,000 in cash to buy Carl his brace. No middleman, no fees — just a community supporting a legend.

The Youth Nationals Mission

In April 2026, we hosted a high-impact fundraiser for youth stars Rhyder and Emma. Through live stream QR codes and donors who paid $100 to watch volunteers shave their heads, we raised $850 for their travel to the May Nationals. We have also raised $4,600 for a grieving mother and supported travel for players to the World Cup in South Korea and Hungary.

"By helping us, you are fueling the future. Our goal is to encourage kids to join the sport, bring their friends, and grow the game until Atlantic Canada is the dominant force in World Darts."
